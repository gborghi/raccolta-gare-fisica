## Problema 2

**Question 2.**

**(a)** A planet has a radius $R$ and the gravitational field strength at its surface is $g$. A satellite is placed in a circular orbit of radius $r$ around the planet.

Obtain an expression for $T$, the period of orbit of the satellite, in terms of $R$, $g$ and $r$, explaining the relevant physics in each of your steps.

(3)

**(b)** Both the Earth and Jupiter can be assumed to have circular orbits about the Sun. Jupiter has an orbital period, $T_\text{Jupiter} = 11.9$ years. What is the orbital radius of Jupiter in au?

Note: an au (astronomical unit) is the average distance between the Earth and the Sun.

(1)

**(c)** The density of the Earth changes sharply between the core and the mantle. By taking just two suitable values of the density of the core and mantle of the Earth from the graph in Figure 1, estimate a value of the mass of the Earth using this data. Your values and calculation must be shown.

(3)

**(d)** A planet of mass $M_p$ has an orbiting moon of mass $M_m$. The mass of the moon is about $\tfrac{1}{8}$ the mass of the planet.

(i) Sketch a set of equipotential lines for the planet–moon system. Ignore the orbital motion of the moon. Without detailed calculation, indicate on your sketch the relative positions of the neutral point (a point of zero field strength) and the centre of mass of the planet–moon system.

A rocket of mass $m$ is launched from the planet. The rocket engine is used very briefly at take-off when it is close to the planet's surface, and it almost immediately reaches speed $v$, after which the fuel is exhausted and there is no further propulsion. It is then in free-fall and will have just enough energy to reach the moon. Assume that you can ignore relative motion of the planet and moon, and ignore air resistance. Obtain expressions and calculate values for:

(ii) The distance, $d$, from the planet's centre, where the speed of the rocket is lowest.

(iii) The launch speed $v$ of the rocket from the planet, in terms of the variables $M_p$, $M_m$, the radius of the planet, $R_p$, and the separation of the centres of the moon and planet, $D$.

For this question part, you may take $M_p = 6.00 \times 10^{24}$ kg, $M_m = 7.50 \times 10^{23}$ kg, $D = 3.84 \times 10^8$ m.

(9)

**(e)** The Earth can be considered to consist of concentric spheres of material, the outer sphere being the crust with a density of $2500$ kg m$^{-3}$. If the mass of the Earth is $M_E = 6.0 \times 10^{24}$ kg, what will be the fractional change in the gravitational field strength when descending to the bottom of a gold mine, a distance $d = 3000$ m below the surface of the Earth?

*Hint: the field inside a hollow spherical shell is zero.*

(4)

**(f)** A planetary sized object similar to the Earth was formed from a very large spherical cloud of interstellar dust collapsing under gravity to form a spherical body of uniform density $\rho$ and radius $R$.

(i) Find an expression for the change in gravitational potential energy as the radius of the proto-planet increases from $r$ to $r + \delta r$ by the formation of a thin spherical shell of material of mass $\delta m$.

(ii) Now derive an expression for the total transfer of gravitational potential energy in the formation of the planet. Use the following terms: the gravitational constant $G$, and the final radius and mass, $R$ and $M$ respectively.

(iii) If all of this energy was used to heat the planet formed at a very low initial temperature, estimate the temperature that the planet would have reached.

For this planet, take $R = 6400$ km, $M = 4.0 \times 10^{26}$ kg, and the average specific heat capacity of the material of the planet is $700$ J kg$^{-1}$ K$^{-1}$.

(5)

## Problema 3

**Question 3.**

**(a)** A particle is moving in a horizontal circle and is kept in its path by a string tied to a point at a height $h$ above the centre of the circle.

(i) Find the period of rotation in terms of $h$ and the gravitational field strength $g$, explaining the physics of your derivation in words.

(ii) If the tension in the string is three times the weight of the particle, find the length of the string $l$, in terms of $h$.

(5)

**(b)**

(i) A particle of mass $m$ is suspended by a thin elastic string of natural length $a$ in which a tension $mg$ would produce an extension $a$. Write down an expression for the period of oscillation of the mass in terms of $a$ and $g$.

(ii) Two particles of equal mass $m$ on a smooth horizontal table are connected by the same thin elastic string of natural length $a$. The particles are held at rest at a distance $3a$ apart.

i. Describe their motions between the time they are released and when they collide.

ii. If the particles are released simultaneously, calculate the time elapsed before they collide, given $a = 0.20$ m.

(7)

**(c)** A piece of elastic cord of length $l_0$ and cross-sectional area $A_0$ is stretched to twice its natural length. Assuming that the total volume remains constant, and that cross-sectional area is uniform along its length, and the Young's modulus $E$ for the material remains constant,

(i) obtain an expression for the final tension in terms of $E$ and $A_0$.

(ii) Show that the work done in stretching the elastic is given by $E l_0 A_0 (1 - \ln 2)$.

(5)

**(d)** The behaviour of the boxing training ball shown in Figure 2 can be modelled as two identical vertical springs, each of natural length $\ell$ and spring constant $k$, attached to a mass $m$. A force $mg$ applied to a single spring doubles its length to $2\ell$. The top spring is attached to the ceiling and the bottom spring to the floor of a room of height $4\ell$.

(i) Determine the equilibrium position of the ball below the ceiling in terms of $\ell$.

(ii) The ball is given a small vertical displacement from equilibrium and released. Find an expression for the period $T_v$ of the vertical oscillations.

(iii) The ball is given a small horizontal displacement $x$ from equilibrium and released. Using a suitable approximation, provide an expression for the horizontal force $F_h$ exerted on the ball by the springs, and hence find the period $T_h$ of the horizontal oscillations.

(8)

## Problema 4

**Question 4.**

**(a)** Show that the pressure at depth $h$ in a liquid of density $\rho$ is given by $p = p_0 + \rho g h$, where $p_0$ is the pressure at the surface.

(2)

**(b)** A large water tank sits on the ground and is kept full of water to a depth $H$. Water issues horizontally from a small circular hole of radius $r$ in the side wall at a height $h$ above the ground.

(i) Using Bernoulli's equation (or otherwise), show that the speed of the water leaving the hole is $v = \sqrt{2g(H-h)}$.

(ii) Show that the water strikes the ground at a horizontal distance $x = 2\sqrt{h(H-h)}$ from the base of the tank.

(iii) Find the value of $h$ for which $x$ is a maximum, and determine this maximum range.

(iv) At what other value of $h$ does the water strike the ground at the same point as in (iii)?

(6)

**(c)** A closed hollow container of volume $V$ and mass $m_c$ is held submerged in water of density $\rho_w$ by a string attached to the bottom of a tank. If the container is just on the point of floating when the string is cut,

(i) obtain an expression for the tension $T$ in the string in terms of $V$, $m_c$, $\rho_w$ and $g$.

(ii) A cylindrical hollow metal container has a diameter $d = 0.25$ m and a height of $0.30$ m, and a total mass $m_c = 3.2$ kg. Calculate the tension $T$.

(4)

**(d)** A uniform rod of length $L$, cross-sectional area $A$, and material density $\rho_r$ floats with a fraction $f$ of its length submerged in a liquid of density $\rho_l$. Show that $f = \rho_r / \rho_l$.

A wooden rod of length $L = 1.0$ m has density $\rho_r = 700$ kg m$^{-3}$. It floats in water ($\rho_l = 1000$ kg m$^{-3}$). What length of the rod is above the water?

(4)

**(e)** A solid circular cone of height $h$, mass $m$, and base radius $r$ floats in a liquid of density $\rho_l$ with its axis vertical and vertex downwards. The cone floats with a height $h/n$ submerged below the liquid surface (where $n > 1$).

The volume of a circular cone is $V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$.

(i) Obtain an expression relating the mass $m$ of the cone to the fraction submerged, in terms of $r$, $h$, $n$, and $\rho_l$.

(ii) A weight equal to the weight of the cone is placed on the base. As this weight is added, the cone sinks until the base is just level with the liquid surface before rising back up. Show that $n^3 + n^2 + n = 7$.

(9)

## Problema 5

**Question 5.**

**(a)** A potential divider circuit consists of two resistors $R_1$ and $R_2$ connected in series across a supply voltage $V_s$. A load of resistance $R_L$ is connected across $R_2$.

(i) Derive an expression for the output voltage $V_\text{out}$ across $R_L$ in terms of $V_s$, $R_1$, $R_2$, and $R_L$.

(ii) For $V_s = 12$ V, $R_1 = 4.0\ \text{k}\Omega$, $R_2 = 8.0\ \text{k}\Omega$, and $R_L = 16\ \text{k}\Omega$, calculate $V_\text{out}$.

(4)

**(b)** A strain gauge has resistance $R_0 = 120\ \Omega$ when unstrained. Its gauge factor is defined as $G = (\Delta R / R_0) / \varepsilon$, where $\varepsilon$ is the strain (fractional change in length) and $\Delta R$ is the change in resistance. $G = 2.0$ for this gauge.

The strain gauge is connected as one arm of a Wheatstone bridge with three equal resistors, each of resistance $R_0$, and a battery of EMF $\mathcal{E} = 6.0$ V with negligible internal resistance.

(i) Calculate the out-of-balance voltage $V_{AB}$ when the strain gauge is under a strain $\varepsilon = 1.0 \times 10^{-3}$.

(ii) A second identical strain gauge, experiencing the same strain but oriented to decrease resistance, is placed in the opposite arm of the bridge. How does this affect $V_{AB}$?

(6)

**(c)** Three resistors of resistance $R$, $2R$, and $3R$ are connected in a network to a battery of EMF $\mathcal{E}$ and negligible internal resistance. Calculate the current supplied by the battery and the power dissipated in each resistor.

(Sketch the network diagram clearly.)

(5)

**(d)** Consider a semi-infinite ladder network of identical resistors, each of resistance $R$, as shown in Figure 5. The network consists of series resistors $R$ and shunt resistors $R$ repeating to infinity.

(i) Show that the input resistance of the network between terminals A and B is $R_{AB} = R(1 + \sqrt{3})/2$.

(ii) A battery of EMF $\mathcal{E} = 10$ V and internal resistance $r = R$ is connected across A and B. Calculate the current drawn from the battery and the voltage at the first shunt resistor.

(6)

**(e)** An array of identical cells, each of EMF $\mathcal{E}_0$ and internal resistance $r_0$, is arranged in $m$ rows and $n$ columns (i.e. $m$ rows each of $n$ cells in series). The array is connected to an external resistance $R$.

(i) Obtain an expression for the current $I$ through $R$.

(ii) Find the condition on $R$, $m$, $n$, and $r_0$ such that the power delivered to $R$ is maximised.

## Problema 6

**Question 6.**

**(a)** Three capacitors of capacitances $C_1 = 2.0\ \mu\text{F}$, $C_2 = 4.0\ \mu\text{F}$, and $C_3 = 6.0\ \mu\text{F}$ are connected in series across a potential difference of $V = 12$ V.

(i) Calculate the charge stored on each capacitor and the potential difference across each.

(ii) Calculate the total energy stored in the combination.

(iii) The three capacitors are now connected in parallel across the same $V = 12$ V. Calculate the total energy stored. Comment on any difference from (ii).

(4)

**(b)** A parallel plate capacitor has plates of area $A$ separated by a distance $d$.

(i) Show that the capacitance is $C = \varepsilon_0 A / d$.

(ii) The capacitor is charged to a potential difference $V_0$ and then disconnected from the supply. The plates are then pulled apart to a separation $2d$. Determine the new potential difference, the new energy stored, and account for any change in energy.

(5)

**(c)** Two concentric conducting spheres have radii $a$ and $b$ ($a < b$). They carry equal and opposite charges $\pm Q$ (inner sphere positive).

(i) Using Gauss's law, find the electric field in the region $a < r < b$, and hence obtain an expression for the capacitance of this system.

(ii) A capacitor of this type has $a = 0.10$ m and $b = 0.11$ m. It is charged to a potential difference of $5.0$ V. Calculate the charge $Q$ on the inner sphere and the energy stored.

(iii) The outer sphere is earthed momentarily and then an additional charge $Q$ is placed on the inner sphere. Determine the new potential difference between the spheres.

(7)

**(d)** A capacitor of capacitance $C = 4000\ \mu\text{F}$ is charged to an initial potential difference $V_0 = 5.0$ V and is then connected in series with a resistor $R$ and an LED. The LED can be considered to have a constant forward voltage drop of $2.7$ V and to be off for voltages below this. A constant current circuit is used to keep the current through the LED at $I_\text{LED} = 10$ mA while the LED is on.

(i) Determine the time for which the LED remains lit after the circuit is switched on.

(ii) Sketch a graph of the capacitor voltage $V_C$ against time, from $t = 0$ until the LED goes off.
