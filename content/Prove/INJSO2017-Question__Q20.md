# INJSO 2017 — Problema 20

P, Q, R are different colourless solids, while S is a colourless solution. They are (in random order) Sodium chloride (NaCl), Calcium Carbonate (CaCO$_3$), Acetic acid (CH$_3$COOH) and Phenolphthalein indicator. Small amount of the above substances were added in pairs (e.g. P with Q, Q with R etc.) to a small amount of water in a test tube. They give the following results as shown in the observation table.

**Observations table:**

| | P | Q | R |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Q** | No reaction | – | No reaction |
| **R** | Dark Pink Colour | No reaction | |
| **S** | No reaction | No reaction | Effervescence |

Then the chemicals are

- (a) P=NaCl, Q=CaCO$_3$, R=CH$_3$COOH, S=Phenolphthalein
- (b) P=Phenolphthalein, Q=NaCl, R=CaCO$_3$, S=CH$_3$COOH
- (c) P=CH$_3$COOH, Q=Phenolphthalein, R=NaCl, S=CaCO$_3$
- (d) P=CaCO$_3$, Q=CH$_3$COOH, R=Phenolphthalein, S=NaCl
