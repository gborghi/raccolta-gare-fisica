# INJSO 2020 — Problema 2

For any conductor, the thermal dependence of resistance is given by $R = R_0[1 + \alpha(\Delta\theta)]$, where $\Delta\theta$ is the temperature difference in °C, $\alpha$ is a constant having the dimensions of $T^{-1}$ and $R_0$ is the resistance of the wire at 0°C.

A wire made of a conductor, with $\alpha < 0$, is subjected to a constant voltage $V$. Then, for the wire, as the time progresses,

- (A) the temperature as well as the current will go on decreasing.
- (B) the temperature will go on decreasing while the current will go on increasing.
- (C) the temperature as well as the current will go on increasing.
- (D) the temperature will go on increasing while the current will go on decreasing.
