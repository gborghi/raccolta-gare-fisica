# INPhO 2024 — Problema 4

**4. Mag-Grav Tussle**

A rectangular conducting loop of mass $m$, width $w$, length $h$, and self inductance $L$ is held in the vertical $x$-$y$ plane with its bottom edge along the $y$-axis (see figure on the left below). In this problem take the resistance of the loop to be zero. The region $x > 0$ contains a uniform magnetic field $\vec{B} = B\hat{z}$ (and $\vec{B} = 0$ for $x \le 0$), pointing out of the plane. The loop is released from rest at time $t = 0$ and descends under gravity (see the figure to the right below). The acceleration due to gravity $g$ is in $+x$ direction.

$$\vec{B} = \begin{cases} B\hat{z} & \text{for } x \le 0 \\ 0 & \text{for } x \ge 0 \end{cases}$$
