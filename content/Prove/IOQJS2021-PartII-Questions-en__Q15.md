# IOQJS 2021 (Part II) — Problema 15

[8 marks] Flame is a hot bright stream of burning gases. Flames have different structures and properties depending on fuel and burning conditions. The attached figure (drawn approximately to scale) shows a candle flame burning in open air in which three regions are distinctly visible surrounding a dark zone: an innermost zone that is pale yellow in colour, surrounded by a red zone, with a bluish envelope at the outside. Combustion of the flame is different in these different locations in the flame. Consider wax to have chemical formula C₂₅H₅₂.

**15.1.** Among points **1 - 6**, identify

- (a) the hottest point.
- (b) the coldest point.
- (c) the point where water vapour concentration is the highest.

**15.2.** From the following list, identify two substances that are present at point **3** but are absent at point **6**. Also write chemical equations for the reactions causing removal of these substances.

*List:* Oxygen, Nitrogen, Carbon, Wax, Carbon dioxide, Carbon monoxide, Water.

**15.3.** The space at point **2** prominently has (identify the correct option):

- (A) only air.
- (B) air with freshly evaporating wax vapour.
- (C) air with extra carbon dioxide released from combustion.
- (D) oxygen rich air (as oxygen concentration has locally increased due to diffusion).

**15.4.** Another flame used in laboratories is produced from a Bunsen burner. It is used for heating, combustion, sterilisation processes, etc. By adjusting the ratio of gas (fuel) and air in a Bunsen burner, it is possible to get a stable blue flame, which is largely non-luminous. Shlok was given two different organic compounds: naphthalene (C₁₀H₈) and citric acid (C₆H₈O₇). He burned 1.0 g of each compound separately in a porcelain piece in a blue Bunsen burner flame.

For which of the two compounds would the flame emit more yellow light? Write reason for your answer, along with necessary supporting calculations/arguments.
