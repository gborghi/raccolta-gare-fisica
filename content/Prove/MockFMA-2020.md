of the water. The density of the rod is a fifth of that of water. At $t = 0$, the rod is dropped from rest
and is just about able to be submerged completely before rising. How long does it take for the rod to
reach its lowest point? Ignore effects from splashing and drag.
(A) $\sqrt{\dfrac{2\ell}{g}} + \pi\sqrt{\dfrac{\ell}{5g}}$
(B) $\sqrt{\dfrac{3\ell}{g}} + \pi\sqrt{\dfrac{\ell}{5g}}$
(C) $\sqrt{\dfrac{2\ell}{g}} + \pi\sqrt{\dfrac{3\ell}{g}}$
(D) $\sqrt{\dfrac{2\ell}{g}} + \dfrac{\pi}{2}\sqrt{\dfrac{3\ell}{g}}$
(E) $\sqrt{\dfrac{3\ell}{g}} + \dfrac{\pi}{2}\sqrt{\dfrac{\ell}{5g}}$
