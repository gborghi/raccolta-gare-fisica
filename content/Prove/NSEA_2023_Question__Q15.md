# NSEA 2023 — Problema 15

In spectroscopy doublet lines are closely spaced spectral lines that arise from transitions from a common fundamental state to states which differ only in their total angular momentum value. If a source of light is moving away from us the light from the source will appear shifted to the longer wavelength (red) side of the spectrum. The shift is quantified by the redshift $z$, where $\lambda_0$ is the observed wavelength of the spectral line when the emitting source is at rest with respect to the observer and $\Delta\lambda$ is the observed shift in the wavelength, so $z = \Delta\lambda/\lambda_0$. Hubble observed that spectral lines of galaxies not very close to us are redshifted. A galaxy is showing a redshift of $z = 0.005$. The observed wavelength separation, in nanometre, of the sodium doublet at 589 and 589.6 nm and of the potassium doublet at 766.5 and 769.9 nm in the spectrum of the galaxy are respectively

- (a) 0.603, 3.417
- (b) 0.003, 0.017
- (c) 38.33, 29.45
- (d) 38.33, 38.5
