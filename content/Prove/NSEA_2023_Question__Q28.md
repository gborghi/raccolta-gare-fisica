# NSEA 2023 — Problema 28

The Lane–Emden equation $\dfrac{1}{\xi^2}\dfrac{d}{d\xi}\left(\xi^2 \dfrac{d\theta}{d\xi}\right) + \theta^n = 0$ is the governing equation for a crude model for a star. Here the star is treated as a sphere of gas with a polytropic equation of state. The differential equation admits exact solutions for polytropic index $n = 0, 1$ and $5$ as $\theta_0 = 1 - \dfrac{\xi^2}{6}$, $\theta_1 = \dfrac{\sin\xi}{\xi}$ and $\theta_5 = \left(1 + \dfrac{\xi^2}{3}\right)^{-1/2}$. The value of $\xi_1$ is such that $\theta(\xi_1) = 0$ defines the surface of the star.

The quantity $\dfrac{\rho_{\text{central}}}{\rho_{\text{average}}} = \dfrac{\xi_1}{3|\theta'(\xi_1)|}$ gives the ratio of the central density to the average density for the model star. $|\theta'|$ means the value of $|d\theta/d\xi|$ evaluated at $\xi = \xi_1$. The values of $\dfrac{\rho_{\text{central}}}{\rho_{\text{average}}}$ for $n = 0, 1$ are

- (a) $1,\, \dfrac{\pi^2}{3}$
- (b) $\sqrt{5},\, \dfrac{\pi}{3}$
- (c) $\dfrac{\pi}{3},\, \pi^2$
- (d) $1,\, \dfrac{\pi}{3}$
