# NSEA 2023 — Problema 52

The rotation period of a spherical moon about its own axis is equal to its period of revolution round its parent planet. Which of the following statements is/are true?

- (a) If the orbital and spin angular momentum vectors of the moon are parallel to each other then, the moon will always show the same portion of its surface to the planet
- (b) If the spin angular momentum vector of the moon lies in its orbital plane, then every portion of the surface of the moon may be seen from the planet
- (c) If the orbital and spin angular momentum vectors of the moon are antiparallel with respect to each other then, viewed from the planet, the moon will appear to be non-rotating
- (d) If the spin angular momentum vector of the moon lies in its orbital plane, then, viewed from the planet, the moon will appear to be rotating with a higher angular speed
