# NSEA 2023 — Problema 56

For vectors $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$ & $\mathbf{C}$ in three dimensional Euclidean space, let $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ stand for the cross product, $[\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B}] = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{B}\mathbf{A}$ for the commutator of $\mathbf{A}$ & $\mathbf{B}$ and $\{\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B}, \mathbf{C}\} = (\mathbf{A}\mathbf{B})\mathbf{C} - \mathbf{A}(\mathbf{B}\mathbf{C})$ for the associator of $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$ & $\mathbf{C}$. Then which of the following relations hold true?

- (a) $\mathbf{A}^2 = \mathbf{0}$ for $\mathbf{A} \neq \mathbf{0}$
- (b) $[\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B}] = \mathbf{0}$ for $\mathbf{A} \parallel \mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B} \neq \mathbf{0}$
- (c) $\{\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B}, \mathbf{C}\} = \mathbf{0}$ for $\mathbf{A} \parallel \mathbf{B} \parallel \mathbf{C}$ and $\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B}, \mathbf{C} \neq \mathbf{0}$
- (d) $\mathbf{A}(\mathbf{B}\mathbf{C}) + \mathbf{B}(\mathbf{C}\mathbf{A}) + \mathbf{C}(\mathbf{A}\mathbf{B}) = \mathbf{0}$ for $\mathbf{A} \parallel \mathbf{B} \parallel \mathbf{C}$ and $\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B}, \mathbf{C} \neq \mathbf{0}$
