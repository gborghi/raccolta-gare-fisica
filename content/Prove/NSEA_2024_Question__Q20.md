# NSEA 2024 — Problema 20

Consider the ellipse with equation $\frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{b} = 1$ with one focus at $(2, 0)$. The equation to the line that passes through the point $(0, 2)$ and is perpendicular to the tangent to the ellipse at $(x_0, y_0)$ is

- (a) $y = \dfrac{9\left(1 - \frac{x_0^2}{9}\right)^{1/2}}{\sqrt{5}\,x_0}\, x + 2$
- (b) $y = \dfrac{9}{10 x_0}\, x + 2$
- (c) $y = -\dfrac{9\sqrt{1 - \frac{x_0^2}{9}}}{\sqrt{5}\,x_0}\, x + 2$
- (d) $y = -\dfrac{9}{10 x_0}\, x + 2$
