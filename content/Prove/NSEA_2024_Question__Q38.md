# NSEA 2024 — Problema 38

In quantum mechanics the wave function carries all the information about the system. The spin part of the general state of a spin half particle, with spin pointing in the $(\theta, \phi)$ direction in three dimensional space can be written down in matrix form as $\chi = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\dfrac{\theta}{2} \\ e^{i\phi}\sin\dfrac{\theta}{2} \end{pmatrix}$. The scalar product between two vectors $\chi$ and $\psi$ is given by $\langle\chi|\psi\rangle$ where $\langle\chi|$ stands for the complex conjugate transpose of $|\chi\rangle$. The vector orthogonal to $\chi$ is

- (a) $\begin{pmatrix} \sin\dfrac{\theta}{2} \\ e^{i\phi}\cos\dfrac{\theta}{2} \end{pmatrix}$
- (b) $\begin{pmatrix} \sin\dfrac{\theta}{2} \\ -e^{i\phi}\cos\dfrac{\theta}{2} \end{pmatrix}$
- (c) $\begin{pmatrix} -\sin\dfrac{\theta}{2} \\ e^{i\phi}\cos\dfrac{\theta}{2} \end{pmatrix}$
- (d) $\begin{pmatrix} -\sin\dfrac{\theta}{2} \\ -e^{i\phi}\cos\dfrac{\theta}{2} \end{pmatrix}$
