# NSEA 2024 — Problema 44

Stars emit radiation approximately like perfectly black bodies. The amount of light energy emitted per unit time is called the luminosity of the star. Luminosity $L$ and radius $R$ of main sequence stars whose masses are neither too high nor too low are related to mass $m$ of star as $L \propto m^{3.5}$ and $R \propto m^{0.8}$. The surface temperature $T$ depends on the mass as

- (a) $T \propto m^{2.1}$
- (b) $T \propto m^{0.475}$
- (c) $T \propto m^{1.1}$
- (d) $T \propto m^{1.275}$
