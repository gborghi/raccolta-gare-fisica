# NSEA 2024 — Problema 53

Calculus was invented by Newton to solve the equations that came up when he examined motion under a central force such as gravity. The force of gravity is the controlling force underlying the laws of Kepler. The possible solutions to the equation were obtained as conic sections. Which of the following is/are possible solutions ($x$, $y$, $t$, $r$, $\theta$ are standard coordinates; $A$, $B$, $k$ and $K$ are constants) to the Kepler problem?

- (a) $x(t) = A\cos(kt)$; $y(t) = B\sin(kt)$
- (b) $r(t) = k$; $\theta(t) = Kt$ modulo $2\pi$
- (c) $x(t) = A\sec\theta$; $y(t) = B\tan\theta$
- (d) $\dfrac{x-2}{2} = \dfrac{y-3}{3} = \dfrac{z-4}{4}$
