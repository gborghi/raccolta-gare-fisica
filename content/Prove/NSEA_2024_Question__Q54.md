# NSEA 2024 — Problema 54

Consider the functions $f$ and $g$ defined by $f(x) = |x - 1|$, $x \in \mathbb{R}$ and $g(x) = \sin x$, $x \in \mathbb{R}$. Define the sum of $f$ and $g$ as $(f+g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)$, $x \in \mathbb{R}$ and difference of $f$ and $g$ as $(f-g)(x) = f(x) - g(x)$, $x \in \mathbb{R}$. Which of the following is/are true?

- (a) $f + g$ is differentiable at $x = 0$
- (b) $f - g$ is not differentiable at $x = 0$
- (c) $f - g$ is not differentiable at $x = 1$
- (d) $f + g$ is differentiable at $x = 1$
