# NSEA 2024 — Problema 56

Consider the LPP

$$\text{Max } Z = x + y \text{ subject to the constraints}$$
$$x + y \leq 6, \quad 2x + 3y \leq 6, \quad x - y \leq 3, \quad x, y \geq 0$$

Which of the following is/are true?

- (a) $x = 3$, $y = 3$ is a feasible solution of the LPP
- (b) $x = 3$, $y = 3$ is an optimal solution of the LPP
- (c) $x = k$, $y = 6 - k$ is an optimal solution of the LPP for $1 \leq k \leq 4$
- (d) The LPP has infinitely many optimal solutions
