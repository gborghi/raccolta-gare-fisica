# NSEA 2025 — Problema 14

Two hunters approach a prey from opposite directions. One hunter has a probability of $\dfrac{1}{3}$ for hitting the prey, the other has a probability of $\dfrac{2}{3}$ for hitting the prey. Both fires a burst of 3 shots each simultaneously. Assume that both the hunters fire one shot each at the same time simultaneously, followed by another shot each at the same time, followed by the third shot each at the same time, each round of shots have negligible time gap between them. The probability that the prey, in question, is hit

- (a) after the three rounds is 1
- (b) in the first round of shots is 1
- (c) after the three rounds is $\dfrac{7}{9}$
- (d) after the three rounds is $\dfrac{721}{729}$
