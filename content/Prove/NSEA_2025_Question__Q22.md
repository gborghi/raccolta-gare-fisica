# NSEA 2025 — Problema 22

Let $T_1$ denote an equilateral triangle of side length $a$, $T_2$ be the triangle whose vertices are midpoints of sides of $T_1$, $T_3$ be the triangle whose vertices are midpoints of sides of $T_2$. We continue in similar manner to obtain the triangles $T_4, T_5, \ldots$. For each natural number $n$, let $P_n$ denote the perimeter of the triangle $T_n$. Then $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} P_n =$

- (a) $12a$
- (b) $8a$
- (c) $6a$
- (d) $3a$
