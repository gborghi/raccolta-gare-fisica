# NSEJS 2023 — Problema 35

It is known that the speed of sound in a gas is directly proportional to the square root of its absolute temperature $T$ measured in Kelvin, i.e. $v \propto \sqrt{T}$. Speed of sound in air at 0°C is 332 m/s. On a hot day, the speed of sound was measured 360 m/s in NCR Delhi; the temperature of air in Delhi on that very day must have been close to

- (a) 40°C
- (b) 42°C
- (c) 44°C
- (d) 48°C
