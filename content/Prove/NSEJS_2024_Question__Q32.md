# NSEJS 2024 — Problema 32

An esterification process has been represented by the following chemical reaction:

$$\text{Ph}\text{O}^{18}\text{H} + \text{H}_3\text{C}\text{OH} \xrightarrow{\text{Conc. H}_2\text{SO}_4} \text{Ester} + \text{Water}$$

The fate of the labelled oxygen ($\text{O}^{18}$) atom in the product is:

- (a) The $\text{O}^{18}$ ends up in the ester as $\text{Ph}\text{O}^{18}\text{CH}_3$
- (b) The $\text{O}^{18}$ ends up in the ester as $\text{Ph}\text{O}\text{CH}_3$ with $\text{O}^{18}$ in the carbonyl
- (c) Mixture of both ester forms
- (d) $\text{H}_2\text{O}^{18}$
