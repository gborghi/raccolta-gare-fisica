# NSEP 2023 — Problema 53

A small dipole is placed at the origin with its dipole moment $\vec{P} = p\,\hat{i}$ oriented along x axis. E and V, are respectively, the Electric field and potential at point A(x, y). The observations at the Point A(x, y) which is at a large distance r from the origin, show that

- (a) $E_x = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\dfrac{p(2x^2 - y^2)}{r^5}$
- (b) $E_x = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\dfrac{p(x^2 - 2y^2)}{r^5}$
- (c) $E_y = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\dfrac{3pxy}{r^5}$
- (d) $V = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\dfrac{\vec{P}\cdot\vec{r}}{r^3}$
