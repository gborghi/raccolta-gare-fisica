# NSEP 2024 — Problema 19

The number density of conduction electrons in pure silicon at room temperature is about $10^{16}$ m$^{-3}$. The number density of conduction electrons is increased by a factor of $10^6$ by doping the silicon lattice with phosphorus. Assume that at room temperature every phosphorus atom contributes one electron to the conduction band. The fraction of silicon atoms replaced by phosphorus atoms is (Given that the density of silicon is 2.33 gm/cm$^3$ and that the molar mass of silicon $M = 28.1$ gm)

- (a) $1.0 \times 10^{-7}$
- (b) $2.0 \times 10^{-7}$
- (c) $4.0 \times 10^{-7}$
- (d) $5.0 \times 10^{-7}$
