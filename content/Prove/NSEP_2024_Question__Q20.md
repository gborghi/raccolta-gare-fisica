# NSEP 2024 — Problema 20

A soap bubble 10 cm in radius, with a film thickness of $\dfrac{10}{3} \times 10^{-6}$ cm, is charged to a potential of 80 V. The bubble bursts and converts into a single spherical drop. Assuming that the soap solution is a good conductor, the potential at the surface of the drop is

- (a) 2 kV
- (b) 4 kV
- (c) 6 kV
- (d) 8 kV
