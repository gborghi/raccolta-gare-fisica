# NSEP 2024 — Problema 29

The number of radioactive nuclei $N$ of a radioactive sample is experimentally measured as a function of time $t$. At $t = 0$, $N(t = 0) = 50{,}000$ and at $t = 10$ s, $N(t = 10\,\text{s}) = 5000 \pm 100$. The half-life of the sample is estimated from these measurements. The error in the estimation of the half-life is approximately [note that for small values of $x$, $\lim x \to 0$, $\ln(1 + x) \approx x$]

- (a) 0.26 s
- (b) 0.15 s
- (c) 0.05 s
- (d) 0.10 s
