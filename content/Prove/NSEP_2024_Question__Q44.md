# NSEP 2024 — Problema 44

A paramagnetic gas at room temperature 27 °C is placed in an external uniform magnetic field of magnitude $B = 1.5$ tesla. The atoms of the gas have magnetic dipole moment $\mu = 1.0\,\mu_B$ where $1\,\mu_B = \dfrac{eh}{4\pi m}$ is Bohr magneton, and $m$ is the mass of electron. The energy difference $\Delta U_B$ between the parallel alignment and the antiparallel alignment of the atom's magnetic dipole moment, with respect to the external field B, is $x \times 10^{-4}$ eV where the value of $x$ is

- (a) 17.4
- (b) 1.74
- (c) 34.8
- (d) 8.7
