# NSEP 2024 — Problema 48

The charge on a small point object at A produces an electric potential $V = 3$ volt at a point P. Because of an unavoidable situation, leakage of the charge from the object at A starts at $t = 0$ at a constant rate of $3\ \mu$Cs$^{-1}$. To maintain the potential of 3 volt at the point P, the object is made to move towards P with a certain velocity $v$. When the point object crosses the point D shown in the figure, it is found to have a charge of $10\ \mu$C and the direction of its velocity is perpendicular to OD. The resulting magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$, at this instant, at the location O (such that $OD = 1.0$ m) is
