# NSEP 2024 — Problema 50

The Earth is revolving around the Sun (mass $M$) in a circular orbit of radius $r$ with a period of revolution $T = 1$ year. Suppose that during the revolution of the Earth, at any instant, the mass of the Sun instantaneously becomes double i.e. $2M$. The correct alternative(s) is/are [Assume that the Sun and the Earth are point masses]

- (a) The period of revolution of the Earth around the Sun now becomes $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ year
- (b) There is no change in angular momentum of the Earth around the Sun
- (c) Minimum distance of the Earth from Sun during revolution is now $\dfrac{r}{3}$
- (d) Maximum speed of the Earth during revolution around the Sun is $3\sqrt{\dfrac{GM}{r}}$
