# NSEP 2024 — Problema 53

A long straight wire, having a radius greater than 4.0 mm, carries a current that is uniformly distributed over its cross-section. The magnitude of magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$ due to the current is 0.28 mT at $r = 4.0$ mm and 0.20 mT at $r = 10.0$ mm, respectively, from the axis of the wire then the

- (a) magnitude of the magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$ at a distance $r = 2.0$ mm from axis is 0.14 mT
- (b) magnitude of the magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$ at $r = 5.0$ mm is greater than that at $r = 6.0$ mm
- (c) current flowing in the wire is $I = 20$ A
- (d) current density in the wire is $J \approx 1.1 \times 10^5$ A/m$^2$
