# NSEP 2024 — Problema 55

One mole of an ideal monoatomic gas, initially at temperature $T$, is compressed to $\dfrac{1}{8}$ of its volume by a piston in a cylinder such that the heat dissipated into the environment is equal to the change in the internal energy of the gas. Then the

- (a) molar heat capacity of the gas is $C = \dfrac{3R}{2}$
- (b) final temperature of the gas is $2\,T$
- (c) work done on the gas is $3\,RT$
- (d) equation of state of the process is $PV^{4/3} = $ constant
