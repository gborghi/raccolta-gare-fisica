# NSEP 2024 — Problema 57

According to Bohr theory, in the ground state of hydrogen atom, an electron revolves in circular orbit of radius $r$ with velocity $v$ and circulation frequency $f$. The magnetic dipole moment of the electronic orbit is $p_m$. The magnetic field produced by the circulating electron at the center of the atom is $\mathbf{B}$. Then for a He$^+$ ion in the state $n = 2$ (electron in the $2^{nd}$ orbit)

- (a) the radius of the orbit is $2r$
- (b) the magnetic dipole moment of the orbit is $2 p_m$
- (c) the frequency of circulation is $\dfrac{f}{2}$
- (d) the magnetic field at the center is $\dfrac{B}{4}$
