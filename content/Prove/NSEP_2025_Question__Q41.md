# NSEP 2025 — Problema 41

For the electric field $E$, in a region of space where a non-uniform, but spherically symmetric distribution of charge has a charge density $\rho(r)$ as
$$\rho(r) = \begin{cases} \rho_0\left(1 - \dfrac{r}{R}\right) & r \leq R \\ 0 & r > R \end{cases}$$
one can say that

- (a) $E = 0$: both at $r = 0$ and $r = R$
- (b) $E \propto r$ for $r < R$ and $E \propto \dfrac{1}{r^2}$ for $r \geq R$
- (c) the magnitude of $E$ increases with $r$ and reaches its maximum at $r = \dfrac{2}{3}R$
- (d) the maximum electric field produced by the given charge distribution is $E_\text{max} = \dfrac{\rho_0 R}{3\varepsilon_0}$
