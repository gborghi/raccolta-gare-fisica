# NSEP 2025 — Problema 50

One end of a long and thin rope, stretched horizontally with a tension $T = 8\ \text{N}$, along $x$ axis, is supporting a weight after passing over a pulley fixed on a vertical pole (see figure). At the other end, a simple harmonic oscillator (a clamped iron rod along the axis of a solenoid fed with AC voltage and oscillating between north and south poles) at $x = 0$, generates a transverse wave of frequency 100 Hz and an amplitude of 2 cm, in the rope. The wave propagates along the rope. The mass per unit length of the rope is 20 g/m. Ignoring the effect of gravity (on the rope), the correct option(s) is/are

<!--fig:start-->
**Quesito 50**
![[NSEP_2025_Question_p13_f25.png]]
<!--fig:end-->

- (a) Wavelength of the transverse wave is 20 cm.
- (b) Maximum magnitude of transverse acceleration of any point on the rope is nearly $800\ \text{ms}^{-2}$.
- (c) If the oscillator produces maximum negative displacement at $x=0$ at time $t_0 = 0$, the equation of the wave can be expressed as $y(x,t) = 0.02\sin[10\pi x - 100\pi t]$ in SI units.
- (d) Tension in the given rope remaining unchanged, if a harmonic oscillator of frequency 200 Hz is used (instead of earlier frequency 100 Hz), the wavelength will be 10 cm.
