# NSEP 2025 — Problema 57

A particle of mass $m$ moves along the $x$ axis with its potential energy as $U(x) = \alpha(x^2 - \beta x)$ where $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are positive constants. The particle is released from rest at $x = x_0 = \sqrt{\beta}$. Then

- (a) $U(x)$ can be expressed as $U(x) = \alpha x_0\left[(x-x_0)^2 + \left(x_0 - \dfrac{x_0^2}{2}\right)\right]$.
- (b) velocity of the particle $v(x)$ as a function of $x$ can be expressed as $v(x) = \sqrt{\dfrac{2\alpha}{m x_0^2}\left[(x-x_0)^2(x+x_0)\right]}$.
- (c) the maximum speed of the particle is $v_\text{max} = \dfrac{\alpha}{\sqrt{2mx_0}}$.
- (d) the total energy of the particle $\text{KE}(x) + U(x)$ is zero.
