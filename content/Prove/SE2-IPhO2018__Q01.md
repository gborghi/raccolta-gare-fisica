The distance between minima, $x$, is quite small. To reduce the error, the total distance $Nx$, with

$22\,x = 49$ mm $\Rightarrow \bar{x} = 2.227$ mm .

B.4
Using previous results, we get
$d = \dfrac{\lambda}{\sin\theta} \simeq \dfrac{\lambda D}{\bar{x}} = \dfrac{650 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{m} \times 1.643\ \text{m}}{2.227 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}} = 4.795 \times 10^{-4}$ m $= 0.480$ mm .
For the uncertainties, we have
$\dfrac{\sigma_d}{d} = \dfrac{\sigma_\lambda}{\lambda} + \dfrac{\sigma_D}{D} + \dfrac{\sigma_{\bar{x}}}{\bar{x}} = \dfrac{10}{650} + \dfrac{0.0087}{1.643} + \dfrac{0.011}{2.227} = 0.02517 \Rightarrow \sigma_d = 0.02517 \times 0.480$ mm $= 0.012$ mm .

D.3
The dimensionless quantity $\varepsilon$ is given by
$\varepsilon = \dfrac{\ell - \ell_0}{\ell_0} = \dfrac{51.0 - 43.7}{43.7} = 0.167$ .
The uncertainty in $\varepsilon$, $\sigma_\varepsilon$, is calculated propagating the uncertainties in the measured length, $\sigma_\ell$ and
$\sigma_{\ell_0}$:
$\dfrac{\sigma_\varepsilon}{\varepsilon} = \dfrac{\sigma_{(\ell-\ell_0)}}{\ell - \ell_0} + \dfrac{\sigma_{\ell_0}}{\ell_0} = \dfrac{\sqrt{\sigma_\ell^2 + \sigma_{\ell_0}^2}}{\ell - \ell_0} + \dfrac{\sigma_{\ell_0}}{\ell_0} = \dfrac{0.2 \times \sqrt{2}}{7.3} + \dfrac{0.2}{43.7} = 0.0433$
Therefore, $\sigma_\varepsilon = 0.0433 \times 0.167 = 0.0072$.
D.3
$\varepsilon = 0.167 \pm 0.007$ .

D.4
One has
$\dfrac{\sigma}{\varepsilon} = \dfrac{F}{\varepsilon S}$ .
In this case, $S = \pi(d/2)^2 = 1.809 \times 10^{-7}\ \text{m}^2$ and $\varepsilon = 0.167$. We also have $1$ gf $= g \times 10^{-3}$ N with
$g = 9.8\ \text{m}\ \text{s}^{-2}$. Therefore, if $F$ is in gram-force units we have
$\dfrac{\sigma}{\varepsilon} = \dfrac{9.8 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{gf}^{-1}\ \text{N}}{0.167 \times 1.809 \times 10^{-7}\ \text{m}^2}\,F = (324293\ \text{gf}^{-1}\ \text{N}\ \text{m}^{-2})\,F$ ,
where $F$ is in gf , and $\sigma$ is in N $\text{m}^{-2}$. Comparing with $\dfrac{\sigma}{\varepsilon} = \beta F$ we get
$\beta = 324293\ \text{gf}^{-1}\ \text{N}\ \text{m}^{-2}$ .
Note that, if we write
$F(t) = F_0 + F_1 e^{-t/\tau_1} + F_2 e^{-t/\tau_2} + F_3 e^{-t/\tau_3} + \cdots$
(1)
and compare with equation
$\dfrac{\sigma}{\varepsilon} = \beta F(t) = E_0 + E_1 e^{-t/\tau_1} + E_2 e^{-t/\tau_2} + E_3 e^{-t/\tau_3} + \cdots$
(2)
we conclude that $E_0 = \beta F_0$ , $E_1 = \beta F_1$, $E_2 = \beta F_2$, etc.
D.4
$\beta = 3.24 \times 10^5\ \text{gf}^{-1}\ \text{N}\ \text{m}^{-2}$ .
0.3pt

D.7
For a single viscoelastic process,
$F = \dfrac{1}{\beta}(E_0 + E_1 e^{-t/\tau_1}) = F_0 + F_1 e^{-t/\tau_1}$ .
Therefore,
D.7
$\dfrac{dF}{dt} = -\dfrac{F_1}{\tau_1} e^{-t/\tau_1}$ ,
where
$F_1 = \dfrac{E_1}{\beta}$ .

$\ln\left(-\dfrac{dF}{dt}\right) = \ln\left(\dfrac{F_1}{\tau_1}\right) - \dfrac{1}{\tau_1} t$ .

D.11
Since
$y(t) = F(t) - F_0 - F_1 e^{-t/\tau_1}$ ,
then
$y(t) = F_2 e^{-t/\tau_2} + F_3 e^{-t/\tau_3} + \cdots, \quad \tau_2 > \tau_3 > \cdots$
At long times, when the contributions from the higher components are small enough, we expect a
linear behaviour for $\ln y(t)$:
$\ln y = \ln F_2 - \dfrac{1}{\tau_2} t$ .
In this case, the $y(t)$ data points become meaningless above 500 s. In the region 200-500 s the graph is
linear and that region can be used to extract the parameters of the second component. The equation
of the straight line is $\ln y_2 = b_2 + m_2 t$. From the graph below,
$m_2 = -(5.65 \pm 0.19) \times 10^{-3} \Rightarrow \tau_2 = \dfrac{1}{-m_2} = 177$ s
$b_2 = 0.33 \pm 0.07 \Rightarrow F_2 = e^{b_2} = 1.39 \Rightarrow E_2 = \beta F_2 = 4.5 \times 10^5$ N $\text{m}^{-2}$ .
