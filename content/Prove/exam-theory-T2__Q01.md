# OII na Teorica — Problema 1

Trapping Ions and Cooling Atoms
In recent decades, trapping and cooling atoms and ions has been a fascinating topic for physicists, with
several Nobel prizes awarded for work in this area. In the rst part of this question, we will explore a
technique for trapping ions, known as the "Paul trap". Wolfgang Paul and Hans Dehmelt received one half
of the 1989 Nobel Prize in Physics for this work. Next, we investigate the Doppler cooling technique, one
of the works cited in the press release for the 1997 Nobel Prize in Physics awarded to Steven Chu, Claude
Cohen-Tannoudji, and William Daniel Phillips "for developments of methods to cool and trap atoms with
laser light".
