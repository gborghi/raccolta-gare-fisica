# INAO Senior 2009 — Problema 4

A copper cube and a wooden cube of volume $10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3$ each are initially at room temperature. They are then moved to an enclosure of ambient temperature $50\ ^\circ\text{C}$. What can we conclude about the temperatures attained by both cubes after 5 hours? [3]

- (a) $T_\text{copper} > T_\text{wood}$ as thermal conductivity of copper is greater than that of wood.
- (b) $T_\text{wood} > T_\text{copper}$ as specific heat capacity of wood is greater than that of copper.
- (c) The temperatures will depend on the interplay between specific heat capacity and thermal conductivity of the materials.
- (d) Both temperatures will be practically the same, as they are in the enclosure for 5 hours.
