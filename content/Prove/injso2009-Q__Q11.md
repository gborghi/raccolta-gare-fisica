# INJSO 2009 — Problema 11

If $M_b$ is the mass of Earth and $R_e$ is the radius of Earth, acceleration due to gravity, $g$ ($=9.8\ \mathrm{m/s^2}$) is given by the equation $g = G M_b/R_e^2$. Now, if $R$ is the radius of a star of mass $M$, and the following four gives the correct equation for the escape velocity from the star such that it is equal to the speed of light $c$?

- (a) $R^2 = \dfrac{2GM}{c^2}$
- (b) $R = \sqrt{\dfrac{2GM}{c}}$
- (c) $\sqrt{R} = \dfrac{2GM}{c^2}$
- (d) $R = \dfrac{2GM}{c^2}$
