# INJSO 2009 — Problema 63

A $100.0$ g sample of $\mathrm{NaCl}$(s) has an initial temperature of $0\,°\mathrm{C}$. A chemist measures the temperature of the sample as it is heated. Heat is added at a constant rate. The heating curve for the sample is shown below.

**Part I.** Determine the temperature range over which the entire sample is a liquid. ($1$ mark)

- (a) $0\,°\mathrm{C}$ to $801\,°\mathrm{C}$
- (b) $801\,°\mathrm{C}$ to $1465\,°\mathrm{C}$
- (c) $0\,°\mathrm{C}$ to $1465\,°\mathrm{C}$
- (d) $1465\,°\mathrm{C}$ to $0\,°\mathrm{C}$

**Part II.** Identify one line segment on the curve where the average K.E of the particles of the NaCl sample is changing. ($2$ marks)

- (a) AB and CD
- (b) BC and DE
- (c) AB and BC
- (d) CD and DE

**Part III.** Identify one line segment on the curve where the NaCl sample is in a single phase and capable of conducting electricity. ($2$ marks)

- (a) AB
- (b) BC
- (c) CD
- (d) DE
