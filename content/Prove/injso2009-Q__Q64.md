# INJSO 2009 — Problema 64

Combustion reaction of glucose ($\mathrm{C_6H_{12}O_6}$) produces carbon dioxide gas. The occurring reaction is:

$$\mathrm{C_6H_{12}O_6(aq) + 6\,O_2(g) \to 6\,CO_2(g) + 6\,H_2O(l)}$$

Data on enthalpy formation:

- $\Delta H_f°$ of glucose (s) $= -1273.3\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$
- $\Delta H_f°\ \mathrm{CO_2(g)} = -393.5\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$
- $\Delta H_f°\ \mathrm{H_2O(g)} = -241.8\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$
- $\Delta H_f°\ \mathrm{H_2O(l)} = -285.8\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$
- $\Delta H_f°\ \mathrm{O_2(g)} = 0\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}$

Universal gas constant, $R = 0.0821\ \mathrm{L\,atm\,mol^{-1}K^{-1}}$. Volume of $1$ mole gas at $25\,°\mathrm{C}$, $1$ atm $= 22.4$ liters. **(Important: Show all your calculation steps clearly)**

1. Calculate the energy produced when $1$ mole of glucose is oxidized. [$\Delta H°$ products $-\Delta H°$ reactants] ($1$ mark)
2. Calculate the volume of air ($25\,°\mathrm{C}$, $1$ atm) needed to oxidize $10.0$ g of glucose (oxygen content in air is $20.0\%$ volume). ($2$ marks)
3. Calculate the volume of dry carbon dioxide gas produced in the combustion of $10.0$ g of glucose at temperature $37\,°\mathrm{C}$ and pressure at $1$ atm. ($PV = nRT$) ($2$ marks)
