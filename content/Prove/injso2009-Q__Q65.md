# INJSO 2009 — Problema 65

Figure 1 shows a graph of $\mathrm{O_2}$ uptake during and immediately after a period of vigorous exercise. To satisfy the energy demands of the exercise aerobically $3\ \mathrm{dm^3}$ of oxygen per minute must be supplied.

After exercise the body continues to breathe in and use extra oxygen. The amount of this oxygen reserve in our blood, tissue fluids, lungs, haemoglobin and myoglobin. In the initial few minutes of exercise muscle fibre use two other sources for ATP production (other than aerobic respiration). They are:

a) Creatine phosphate system. Muscle cells have $2$–$4$ times as much creatine phosphate as ATP. This is the main source of energy during short bursts of activity.

b) Anaerobic respiration which operates faster than aerobic respiration and provides energy for about $40$–$90$ s.
