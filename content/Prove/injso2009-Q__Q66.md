# INJSO 2009 — Problema 66

**(A)** Diagram 1, Diagram 2, Diagram 3 ... Diagram $n$.

<!--fig:start-->
**Quesito 66**
![[injso2009-Q_p16_f1.png]]
<!--fig:end-->

$3$ dots, $1$ triangle; $4$ dots, $3$ triangles; $5$ dots, $6$ triangles. Complete the table below for $n^{\text{th}}$ diagram. ($3$ marks)

| Diagram | 1 | 2 | 3 | ... | n |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Number of dots | 3 | 4 | 5 | ... | |
| Number of triangles | 1 | 3 | 6 | ... | |

**(B)** If '$S$' is the surface area and cuboid of dimensions $x$, $y$, $z$ and $V$ is the volume, then $1/S$ is equal to ($2$ marks)

- (a) $\dfrac{V}{2}(x + y + z)$
- (b) $\dfrac{1}{2V\left(\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{y}+\frac{1}{z}\right)}$
- (c) $\dfrac{V}{x+y+z}$
- (d) $\dfrac{x+y+z}{V}$
