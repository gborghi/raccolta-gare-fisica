# INJSO 2014 — Problema 64

(10 %) The mass percent of $\text{MnO}_2$ in a sample of a mineral is determined by reacting it with a measured excess of $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$ in acid solution, and then titrating the remaining $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$ with standard $\text{KMnO}_4$. A 0.225 g sample of the mineral is ground and boiled with 75.0 mL of 0.0125 M $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$ solution containing 10mL of concentrated sulfuric acid. After the reaction is complete, the solution is cooled, diluted with water, and titrated with 2.25 ×10⁻² M $\text{KMnO}_4$, requiring 16.00ml to reach the endpoint.

*Note: 5 mol of $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$ react with 4mol of $\text{MnO}_2$.*

**i.** Write a balanced equation for the reaction of $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$ with $\text{MnO}_2$ in acid solution. The products are $\text{Mn}^{2+}$ and $\text{AsO}_4^{3-}$. **[1Mark]**

**ii.** Calculate the number of moles of
i. $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$ added initially. **[1 Mark]**
ii. $\text{MnO}_4$ used to titrate the excess $\text{As}_2\text{O}_3$. **[1 Mark]**
iii. $\text{MnO}_2$ in the sample. **[1 Mark]**

**iii.** Determine the mass percent of $\text{MnO}_2$ in the sample. **[0.5 Marks]**

**iv.** Describe how the endpoint is reflected in the $\text{KMnO}_4$ titration **[0.5 Marks]**
