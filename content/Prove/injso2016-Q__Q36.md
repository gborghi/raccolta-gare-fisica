# INJSO 2016 — Problema 36

**A.** One of the popular astronomical objects is a black hole, having enormous density and hence enormous gravity. The gravity inside a boundary around a black hole is so immense that even light cannot escape from within this boundary (that is why these objects are called black hole). This boundary is called the event horizon. The event horizon is spherical. Radius of the event horizon of a black hole is known as Schwarzschild radius.

Schwarzschild radius depends upon mass (*m*) of the black hole, universal gravitational constant (*G*) and speed of light (*c*) in vacuum.

**I)** Determine *x*, *y* & *z* if the Schwarzschild radius of event horizon is given by $r = G^x\, c^y\, m^z$.
**II)** Calculate Schwarzschild radius of the earth (mass of the earth = $6 \times 10^{24}$ kg) if it gets converted into a black hole. By how much will the gravitational force between the earth and the moon change? [2.0]

**B.** Milind and Prashant sit at *x* = 0 when he sees Milind at *x* = 6 m. Prashant now begins to run towards Milind with 5 m/s, while Milind begins to run *towards* Prashant with $a = 2$m/s².

**I)** When and where will they cross? Also plot their displacement – time plots together.
**II)** Prove that Prashant will cross (meet) Milind twice if Milind accelerates away from Prashant (from his original position, at t = 0) with $a = 2$ m/s². Also plot their displacement time plots together. [4.0]

[Total = 6 marks]
