# INPhO 2011 — Problema 7

**The Cubic Potential**: Consider a particle of mass $m$ moving in one dimension under the influence of potential energy

$$u(x) = \frac{m\omega^2 x^2}{2} - \delta x - \frac{\alpha x^3}{3}$$

Here $\omega$, $\delta$ and $\alpha$ are real and positive.

[ **6 + 3 = 9** ]

(a) Sketch typical plots of $u(x)$ and identify extrema if any.

(b) Consider the case where (in appropriate units) we have $m = 1$, $\omega = \sqrt{2}$, $\alpha = 1$ and $\delta = 1/2$. Sketch the potential energy $u(x)$. If the total energy of the particle moving in this one-dimensional potential is $E = 0$ (in same units), discuss the motion of the particle in terms of allowed regions, boundedness and periodicity.
