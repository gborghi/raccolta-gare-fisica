During the launch phase, the rockets and their payloads are exposed to enormous loads. The aerodynamic load due to friction in the atmosphere plays an essential role. As a simple model, consider a rocket with a cone-shaped tip that has a diameter d and an opening angle of α at the cone tip. The rocket flies at a velocity v through the atmosphere, which at the current altitude has a density of ρatm​. You may assume that the motion of the air molecules in the atmosphere is negligible compared to the rocket velocity.
