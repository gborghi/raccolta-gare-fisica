17 marks Boron Compounds through the Ages

Boron has been widely found in nature in form of Borax, known as “Buraq” in Arabic, suhaga in Hindi, and tankan in Sanskrit. In 1807, Sir Davy isolated the element boron from boric acid. However for another century, synthetic chemists found no interest in boron. Around 1910 in Germany, Alfred Stock prepared a series of compounds called boranes, which had high reactivities with many substances. Around World War II, chemists found boranes could be used for chemical storage of hydrogen for weather balloons and military needs. A popular compound for this purpose was NaBH4 which reacted with water to give hydrogen. 2.1 The gas obtained by reacting 100 g of NaBH4 with water at 25oC was used to fill a balloon.
